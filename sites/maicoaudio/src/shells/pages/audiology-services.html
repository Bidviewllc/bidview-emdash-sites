Hearing Evaluations

We offer complete hearing evaluations to better understand your hearing health. Our tests help determine the type and level of hearing loss you may be experiencing, so we can recommend solutions that are tailored to your lifestyle and needs.

Which hearing test is best for me?

The best hearing test depends on your individual needs. A comprehensive hearing evaluation performed by an audiologist is the most accurate way to assess your hearing health. A professional hearing test is the most reliable way to detect hearing loss and find the best solution for your hearing needs.

Can I test my own hearing at home?

You can do a simple self-check by watching for signs like:

  • Struggling to follow conversations
  • Turning up the volume on your TV or phone
  • Frequently asking others to repeat themselves


There are also online tools and hearing apps that offer basic screenings. While these may give you a general idea of your hearing ability, please note that they arenâ€™t a substitute for a full evaluation. If you notice any signs of hearing loss, schedule a professional hearing test with an audiologist or hearing aid specialist to get accurate answers.

Are online hearing tests accurate?

Online hearing tests can be a helpful first step, but they have limitations. Most measure your ability to hear tones through headphones or speakers, but they donâ€™t evaluate how well you understand speech, middle or inner ear function, and whatâ€™s causing your hearing loss. While online tests may suggest a hearing issue, a professional hearing evaluation is the best way to diagnose the problem and find the right treatment plan.

Sensorineural Hearing Loss Evaluation & Treatment

Our experienced audiologists or hearing instrument specialists at Maico Audiological Services will perform a thorough hearing evaluation to determine the degree and type of hearing loss.

Can sensorineural hearing loss be treated?

While sensorineural hearing loss usually canâ€™t be reversed medically or surgically, it can be effectively managed. The most common treatment is hearing aids, which amplify sound and improve clarity. At Maico Audiological Services, our audiologist or hearing instrument specialist will help find the right hearing aid based on your hearing test results, lifestyle, and communication needs.

How do I know if I have sensorineural hearing loss?

You might notice symptoms like muffled speech, trouble understanding conversations (especially in crowds), or turning up the TV volume often. The only way to know for sure is through a professional hearing evaluation.

Is sensorineural hearing loss the same in both ears?

Not always. Sensorineural hearing loss can affect one ear (unilateral) or both ears (bilateral). It may develop gradually over time or happen suddenly. Some people experience more severe hearing loss in one ear than the other. A comprehensive hearing evaluation at Maico Audiological Services will identify the pattern and severity of your hearing loss, so we can recommend the most effective solution for you.
