What is Tinnitus?

Tinnitus is the perception of sound in your ears when there is no external noise present. People describe it in different ways: ringing, buzzing, hissing, clicking, or even roaring. The sound may come and go, or it may be persistent. For many, tinnitus is linked to hearing loss, but it can also be connected to other health issues.

Tinnitus Support Options

Although there is currently no permanent cure for tinnitus, there are many ways to reduce symptoms and improve your quality of life. At Maico Audiological Services, we offer:

Hearing Aids

If you also have hearing loss, hearing aids can make a significant difference. By amplifying surrounding sounds, they reduce the contrast between environmental noise and the internal ringing. Many modern hearing aids offer tinnitus support, with apps and features which play soothing background sounds to help shift focus away from tinnitus.

Lifestyle Changes

Simple lifestyle adjustments can help reduce tinnitus severity:

  • Limit caffeine and alcohol intake
  • Protect your ears in noisy environments
  • Practice relaxation or stress management techniques
  • Keep a consistent sleep routine

Additional Care

If tinnitus persists or worsens, we may recommend a referral to an ENT (ear, nose, and throat) physician for further evaluation. Working together, we can help ensure you receive the best possible care.

Frequently Asked Questions About Tinnitus

Will tinnitus go away on its own?

Sometimes tinnitus improves over time, especially if itâ€™s caused by temporary factors such as noise exposure or earwax. However, if it lasts more than a few weeks, an evaluation is recommended.

Can stress make tinnitus worse?

Yes. Stress and anxiety can heighten your awareness of tinnitus, making it seem louder. Relaxation techniques and stress management can often help reduce its impact.

Is tinnitus always related to hearing loss?

Not always. While many people with tinnitus also have hearing loss, it can occur even in those with normal hearing. A hearing test will determine whether hearing loss is a factor.

Do hearing aids really help?

Yes. Hearing aids improve overall sound clarity and often include features designed to mask tinnitus, making it less noticeable.

Can earwax cause tinnitus?

Yes. Blocked ear canals can cause ringing or buzzing. Professional removal often provides quick relief.
