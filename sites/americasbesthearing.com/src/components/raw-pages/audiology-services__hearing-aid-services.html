At America’s Best Hearing, our audiologists and hearing instrument specialists work with you to select, fit, and maintain hearing aids that match your individual needs, lifestyle, and budget.

Serving locations across Michigan, Minnesota, and Florida, our team at America’s Best Hearing is here to provide expert, compassionate care to give you the best hearing experience possible.

Benefits of Professional Hearing Aid Services

  • Improved Communication – Hear conversations clearly, even in challenging listening environments.
  • Enhanced Quality of Life – Enjoy music, laughter, and everyday sounds with greater ease.
  • Better Cognitive Function – Untreated hearing loss has been linked to memory concerns. By wearing hearing aids, your brain will have lesser fatigue in picking up sounds and have more time to be engaged.
  • Better Human Connections- Hearing aids can make listening easier and help you stay connected with family, friends, and colleagues. No more shying away from social functions.

Hearing Aid Selection

Choosing the right device depends on your hearing loss, lifestyle, and personal preferences.

Our audiologist or hearing instrument specialists will take the time to explain all your options, including different styles, features, and technology levels.

Our team at America’s Best Hearing will be happy to help you select a device that delivers comfort, clarity, and confidence.

Hearing Assessments

A thorough hearing evaluation is the foundation of any successful hearing aid fitting. Our tests may include:

  • Pure-Tone Audiometry – Measures your ability to hear tones at different pitches and volumes.
  • Speech Audiometry – Evaluates how well you understand words in both quiet and noisy settings.
  • Tympanometry – Checks the health of your middle ear and eardrum.

Hearing Aid Styles

We offer a variety of hearing aid styles to suit different needs:

  • Completely-in-the-Canal (CIC) – Nearly invisible, best for mild to moderate loss.
  • In-the-Canal (ITC) – Small, discreet, but with more features than CIC.
  • In-the-Ear (ITE) – Larger and easier to handle, with more power and longer battery life.
  • Behind-the-Ear (BTE) – Versatile, suitable for all degrees of hearing loss, and simple to adjust.

Hearing Aid Fittings

Once you’ve chosen your hearing aids, our audiologist or hearing instrument specialist will adjust them to ensure a precise, comfortable fit. A good fit not only improves sound quality but also reduces discomfort and feedback.

Programming and Adjustments

Your hearing needs may change over time. America’s Best Hearing audiologists and hearing instrument specialists are here for ongoing support. We will adjust your devices for new environments, improving speech clarity, and ensuring background noise stays manageable.

Counseling and Education

Learning to use hearing aids takes time, and we’re here to guide you. We provide:

  • Communication tips for everyday situations
  • Instructions on care and maintenance
  • Support for managing hearing loss in social or work settings

Real Ear Measurements

We use real ear measurement (REM) testing to confirm that your hearing aids are programmed correctly for your ears. A small microphone is placed in your ear to measure sound levels in real time, ensuring your devices deliver the most accurate amplification possible.

Hearing Aid Repairs & Maintenance

Hearing aids may need attention from time to time. At America’s Best Hearing, we provide:

  • Cleaning and routine maintenance
  • Repairs for most major brands
  • Troubleshooting for common issues
  • Assistance with devices purchased elsewhere

For complex problems, we may send your hearing aid to the manufacturer for repair.

Tips for Care:

  • Keep devices dry and clean
  • Store them in a case when not in use
  • Replace batteries or charge them regularly
  • Schedule yearly check-ups to keep them performing their best
