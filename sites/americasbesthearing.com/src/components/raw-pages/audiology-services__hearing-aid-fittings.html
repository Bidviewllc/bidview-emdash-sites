America’s Best Hearing provides expert hearing aid fittings in Michigan, Minnesota, and Florida.

A hearing aid fitting is more than just placing devices in your ears. It’s a personalized process where our audiologists and hearing instrument specialists carefully adjust your hearing aids to match your unique hearing needs.

Professional hearing aid fittings ensure that speech sounds crisp, background noise is managed, and everyday listening feels as natural and comfortable as possible.

Why is a Professional Hearing Aid Fitting Important?

Hearing aids do far more than make sounds louder. They need to be programmed to your specific hearing loss for the best results. A professional fitting helps you hear speech more clearly in both quiet and noisy environments. You won’t also have to deal with discomfort from sounds that are too loud or distorted With the right fitting, your hearing aids become an effective tool to reconnect you with the sounds you’ve been missing.

Why Should an Audiologist or Hearing Specialist Fit Your Hearing Aids?

Audiologists and hearing instrument specialists are trained to understand how hearing loss affects your daily life. They have the expertise to:

  • Adjust hearing aids using advanced software and real-ear measurements
  • Ensure the devices fit comfortably and securely
  • Teach you how to use and care for your hearing aids properly

A professional fitting prevents issues like discomfort, poor sound quality, or frustration that can come from improperly adjusted devices. Reach out to America’s Best Hearing to schedule an appointment with audiologists and hearing instrument specialists in Florida, Michigan, and Minnesota.

What Happens During a Hearing Aid Fitting?

When you come to America’s Best Hearing, your fitting may include:

  1. Reviewing your hearing test results – Understanding your type and level of hearing loss.
  2. Choosing the right hearing aid – Selecting a device that fits your lifestyle, preferences, and budget.
  3. Programming the hearing aids – Using software to adjust settings to your hearing profile.
  4. Real-ear measurement (REM) – Testing sound levels inside your ear canal to ensure accuracy.
  5. Hands-on training – Learning how to insert, remove, and care for your hearing aids.
  6. Follow-up planning – Scheduling additional visits for adjustments as you get used to your devices.

What to Expect

Your first fitting is an exciting step toward better hearing. At first, some sounds may seem new or louder than expected. Over time, your brain will adjust, and these sounds will blend naturally into your daily life. Adjusting to Hearing Aids

Most people need a few weeks to fully adjust. For starters, we encourage you to wear your hearing aids consistently. You can also practice listening in quiet environments before tackling noisy places. Be patient with yourself. Remember, your brain is learning to hear again.

Follow-Up Appointments

Follow-up visits are a vital part of the fitting process at America’s Best Hearing. If your hearing aids sound too loud, too soft, or uncomfortable, we can fine-tune them. These adjustments ensure your devices continue to perform at their best as you adapt to them.

Hearing Aid Fitting FAQs

Are hearing aid fittings covered by insurance?

Coverage varies by provider. Traditional Medicare generally does not cover hearing aids, but some Medicare Advantage plans, private insurance, and Veterans Affairs benefits may help. Our team can assist you in reviewing your coverage.

How can I make my hearing aid fit better?

If your device feels uncomfortable, your audiologist can adjust it. Custom earmolds or different ear tips may also improve the fit. Never force a hearing aid into your ear—it should feel secure but comfortable.

How long does a hearing aid fitting take?

The first fitting usually takes about an hour. It may take a few follow-up visits over several weeks to fine-tune your devices for the best performance.
