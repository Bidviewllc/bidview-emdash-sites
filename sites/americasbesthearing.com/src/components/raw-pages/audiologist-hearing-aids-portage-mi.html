Frequently Asked Questions

Can’t find the answers you’re looking for? Contact Us.

How do I know if I need a hearing aid?

If you often struggle to follow conversations (especially in noisy settings), regularly turn up the TV or radio, or feel like you’re missing parts of social interactions, it may be time for a hearing evaluation. At America’s Best Hearing, our audiologists can test your hearing and let you know if hearing aids are the right solution for your needs.

Can I get a hearing aid without a hearing test?

Over-the-counter (OTC) hearing aids can be purchased without a hearing test and are designed for adults with mild to moderate hearing loss. However, a professional hearing evaluation at America’s Best Hearing ensures your hearing devices are tailored to your exact needs and helps rule out medical concerns that may require treatment.

How long do hearing aids last?

On average, hearing aids last about 3 to 7 years. Their lifespan depends on the model, how frequently they’re used, and how well they’re maintained. With regular cleanings, proper care, and check-ups, your devices can stay in great working order longer. Many patients also choose to upgrade sooner to take advantage of new features and advancements in technology.

What is the difference between hearing aids and personal sound amplification products (PSAPs)?

Hearing aids are FDA-regulated medical devices designed to treat hearing loss and are custom programmed by a professional to fit your unique hearing needs. PSAPs, on the other hand, are over-the-counter amplifiers intended for people without hearing loss. They make sounds louder but do not offer the same level of precision, personalization, or professional support.
