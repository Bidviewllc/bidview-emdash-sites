Hearing Aid Brands We Work With

Phonak Hearing Aids

Phonak has more than 75 years of experience making hearing aids. Popular models like Phonak Lumity improve speech understanding in noise, while Phonak Naída is designed for people with more severe hearing loss. Most Phonak hearing aids offer Bluetooth streaming, rechargeable batteries, and water-resistant designs.

Oticon Hearing Aids

Oticon has established its roots in hearing care since 1904. Currently, their BrainHearing™ technology works with the way your brain processes sound, making speech clearer and reducing listening effort. Models like Oticon Real and Oticon More also offer tinnitus support and wireless connectivity.

Widex Hearing Aids

Widex is known for its clear, natural sound. Models like Widex Moment and Widex SmartRIC reduce delays in sound and make conversations feel lifelike. Widex also offers excellent options for people with tinnitus, with sound therapy programs and rechargeable models.

ReSound Hearing Aids

ReSound has more than 75 years of innovation. Their hearing aids, including ReSound Omnia and ReSound ONE, are designed for natural sound quality and strong Bluetooth performance. Many ReSound devices also provide personalized sound direction and tinnitus support.

Starkey Hearing Aids

Starkey is a U.S.-based company that combines hearing technology with health features. Starkey Genesis AI and Evolv AI offer fall detection, activity tracking, tinnitus support, and hands-free calling. Starkey also offers rechargeable batteries and smartphone connections make them easy to use.

Signia Hearing Aids

Signia hearing aids are sleek, modern, and packed with features. Devices like Signia Pure Charge&Go and Silk X include Bluetooth streaming, rechargeable batteries, and excellent speech clarity in noise. Unitron Hearing Aids

Unitron focuses on comfort and flexibility. Models like Unitron Blu automatically adjust to different listening environments and include Bluetooth streaming and rechargeable options. Their simple smartphone app makes it easy to fine-tune your hearing aids.

Assistive Listening Devices (ALDs)

Assistive Listening Devices (ALDs) make it easier to hear in certain situations, even if you already use hearing aids. They are helpful for listening to the TV, talking on the phone, or following conversations in groups. Common types include:

  • TV Streamers – Send sound directly from your TV to your hearing aids.
  • FM Systems – Transmit a speaker’s voice straight to your ears in classrooms or meetings.
  • Personal Amplifiers – Portable devices that boost nearby sounds.
  • Hearing Loop Systems – Pick up sound signals in theaters, churches, and public places.
  • Bluetooth Microphones – Help you hear one person clearly in noisy settings.
  • CaptionCall Phones – Provide free real-time captions on a screen while you talk on the phone (requires certification from a hearing professional).
  • CapTel Phones – Similar to CaptionCall, CapTel phones display captions of conversations so you can read what’s being said while you listen.

ALDs help reduce listening effort and keep you connected at home, at work, and in public spaces.

FAQs – Hearing Aids & Products

What is the most advanced hearing aid?

The most advanced hearing aids include speech clarity features, Bluetooth streaming, rechargeable batteries, and strong noise reduction. The best one depends on your hearing test and daily needs.

Which hearing aids are best for tinnitus?

Brands like Widex, Oticon, and Starkey include tinnitus sound therapy features. Our team can help match you with the best option.

Which hearing aids are best for severe hearing loss?

Phonak, Oticon, and Starkey all make powerful hearing aids for people with severe to profound hearing loss. Your audiologist will help find the right solution for you.

How long do custom ear molds last?

Custom ear molds usually last 2 to 5 years with proper care.

Are ALDs required in public places?

Yes. Many theaters, churches, schools, and government buildings are required to provide ALDs to help people with hearing loss access sound more easily.
