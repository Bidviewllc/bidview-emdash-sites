Audiology Services

Table of Contents

Hearing Assessments

A hearing assessment provides a clear understanding of your hearing health and how it affects daily communication. We perform thorough diagnostic testing and take time to explain your results, so you leave with clarity and confidence about next steps.

Which hearing test is the best?

There isn’t a single “best” hearing test for everyone. The best hearing test is the one that matches your symptoms, concerns, and listening challenges.

For most patients, the most accurate and useful approach is a comprehensive audiological evaluation performed by an audiologist. This type of evaluation does not rely on just one test. Instead, it combines multiple tests to provide a complete picture of hearing health, speech understanding, and how hearing issues affect daily life.

How to know if I need a hearing test?

People often need a hearing test if conversations feel unclear, background noise is harder to manage, or listening feels tiring.

How to prepare for a hearing evaluation?

Preparing for a hearing evaluation involves noting symptoms, medical history, and any situations where hearing feels challenging.

Sensorineural Hearing Loss Evaluation & Treatment

Sensorineural hearing loss occurs when the inner ear or auditory nerve is affected and often develops gradually. We evaluate the cause and degree of hearing loss and recommend personalized treatment options designed to improve clarity and communication.

What are the most common signs of sensorineural hearing loss?

Sensorineural hearing loss generally makes speech sound muffled or unclear, especially in group settings or in noisy places. You might feel like people you are talking with are mumbling or notice that you’re turning up the TV more than usual. If conversations feel tiring or you’re missing important sounds, it may be time for a professional evaluation with an audiologist.

How is sensorineural hearing loss treated?

Treatment commonly includes hearing aids or other hearing technology selected based on hearing needs and lifestyle.

How to tell if sensorineural hearing loss is getting worse?

Worsening symptoms often include increased difficulty understanding speech, especially in noise, and higher listening fatigue.

Ear Wax Removal

Excess ear wax can block sound and cause discomfort, hearing changes, or tinnitus symptoms. Professional ear wax removal is a safe and gentle way to restore comfort and improve hearing clarity.

How do I know if ear wax is causing hearing problems?

Ear wax buildup often causes muffled hearing, a feeling of fullness in the ear, ringing sensations, or sudden changes in hearing clarity.

How is professional ear wax removal different from home methods?

Professional ear wax removal uses specialized tools and techniques that safely clear wax without pushing it deeper into the ear canal.

How often should ear wax be removed?

The frequency depends on how quickly wax builds up, but many patients benefit from periodic professional cleanings.

Tinnitus Evaluation

A tinnitus evaluation focuses on understanding how ringing or buzzing affects your hearing, focus, and quality of life. We identify contributing factors and use detailed testing to guide personalized management options.

Why should tinnitus be evaluated by an audiologist?
An audiologist evaluates hearing function, sound perception, and contributing factors that influence how tinnitus is experienced.
How do I know if my tinnitus needs professional care?
Tinnitus often benefits from professional care if it interferes with sleep, concentration, emotional well-being, or daily activities.
How long does a tinnitus evaluation take?
Most tinnitus evaluations take about one hour, depending on testing needs and discussion time.

Tinnitus Treatments

Tinnitus treatment focuses on reducing the daily impact of ringing or buzzing rather than eliminating sound entirely. Our treatment plans may include sound therapy, hearing technology, and education tailored to individual needs.
Can tinnitus be treated even if it cannot be cured?
Tinnitus can often be managed effectively using personalized strategies that reduce awareness and distress.
How effective are tinnitus treatments?

Many patients experience noticeable improvement in comfort, focus, and quality of life with consistent treatment.

How long does tinnitus treatment take to work?

Improvement typically occurs gradually over weeks or months as the brain adapts to management strategies.

Hearing Aid Services

At Raleigh Hearing and Tinnitus Center, our hearing aid services are designed to support you well beyond the initial fitting. We provide personalized hearing aid selection, precise fittings, ongoing adjustments, repairs, and routine maintenance to help your devices perform comfortably and reliably in your everyday life. Our team takes the time to understand your needs and provide continued support, so your hearing technology works for you today and for the long term.

Hearing Aid Fittings

A properly fitted hearing aid can make a meaningful difference in how comfortably and confidently you hear each day. At Raleigh Hearing and Tinnitus Center, we take a personalized approach to hearing aid fittings, carefully programming and fine-tuning your devices using real-ear measurements and individualized adjustments. Our focus is on helping your hearing aids deliver clear, natural sound that fits your hearing needs and your lifestyle.
How long does it take to get fitted for a hearing aid?

A hearing aid fitting appointment typically takes about one to two hours. During this visit, we review your hearing evaluation, discuss the hearing aid options best suited to your needs, and program your devices to your personalized hearing profile. We also spend time showing you how to use, insert, remove, and care for your hearing aids comfortably. Follow-up visits are scheduled to fine-tune settings and support your adjustment over time.

How does an audiologist fit a hearing aid?

The fitting process begins with a detailed review of your hearing test results and listening goals. Based on this information, your audiologist selects and programs hearing aids using precise real-ear measurements to match amplification levels to your ears. The physical fit is adjusted for comfort, and you receive hands-on guidance for daily use, cleaning, and maintenance so you feel confident using your devices.

Can you get a hearing aid without a test?

Over-the-counter hearing aids are available without a hearing test, but they do not provide customized programming or professional support. At Raleigh Hearing and Tinnitus Center, we recommend starting with a comprehensive hearing evaluation. This allows us to identify your specific hearing needs, recommend appropriate solutions, and rule out medical or structural concerns that could affect hearing outcomes.

Hearing Protection for Musicians

Musicians need protection that lowers volume without distorting sound quality. Custom musician hearing protection preserves clarity and balance while reducing the risk of noise-induced hearing loss.

Do musicians really need hearing protection?

Musicians are at higher risk for hearing loss due to prolonged exposure to amplified sound and repeated performances.

How can musicians protect their hearing without losing sound quality?

Custom musician earplugs maintain tonal balance while reducing harmful volume levels.

How long does musician hearing protection last?

With proper care, custom musician earplugs can last several years.

Hearing Aid Repair and Cleaning

Hearing aids are used every day, and regular care plays an important role in keeping them working properly. We provide professional hearing aid cleaning, troubleshooting, and repair services to help your devices stay clear, comfortable, and dependable. Whether you need routine maintenance or help with an unexpected issue, our team is here to support you and minimize interruptions to your hearing.

What are the common problems that require hearing aid repair?

Common hearing aid issues include weak or no sound, distorted or unclear audio, feedback or whistling, and physical wear or damage. Moisture and ear wax buildup can also affect performance. If you notice changes in sound quality or function, our team can evaluate your devices and recommend the appropriate solution.

How long does it take to repair a hearing aid?

Repair time depends on the issue. Many concerns, such as cleaning, replacing filters, or adjusting components, can often be handled in the office, sometimes the same day. More complex repairs that require manufacturer service may take several days to a couple of weeks. We explain the process and keep you informed throughout.

Can I fix my hearing aid at home?

Some basic troubleshooting, such as checking the battery, cleaning openings, or replacing a wax filter, can help resolve minor issues. However, more involved problems should be handled by a professional to avoid further damage. If at-home steps do not help, bringing your hearing aids to Raleigh Hearing and Tinnitus Center allows us to safely assess and restore their performance.

Aural Rehabilitation

Aural rehabilitation focuses on improving listening skills, communication strategies, and confidence after hearing changes. It supports better outcomes with hearing aids and overall communication success.
Who benefits from aural rehabilitation?

Aural rehabilitation benefits individuals adjusting to hearing loss, hearing aids, or changes in listening environments.

How does aural rehabilitation improve daily communication?

It helps patients strengthen listening skills, manage challenging situations, and improve overall communication confidence.

How long does aural rehabilitation last?

The length varies, but many programs span several weeks or months based on individual goals.

Hearing Protection for Hunters

Hunting exposes ears to sudden impulse noise that can cause permanent damage. Specialized hearing protection reduces gunshot noise while allowing environmental awareness.

Why is hearing protection important for hunters?

Gunfire produces impulse noise levels that can cause permanent hearing damage from a single exposure.

How can hunters protect their hearing while staying aware?

Electronic or custom hearing protection reduces gunshot noise while allowing environmental sounds to remain audible.

How damaging is gunfire to hearing without protection?

A single gunshot can cause permanent hearing loss or tinnitus when hearing protection is not used.
