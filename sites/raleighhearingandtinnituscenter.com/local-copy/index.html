How do I know if I need a hearing aid?

If conversations are becoming harder to follow (especially in groups or noisy environments) or if you’re frequently turning up the TV or asking others to repeat themselves, these may be signs of hearing changes. Some patients also notice increased listening fatigue or frustration during social situations. A comprehensive hearing evaluation at Raleigh Hearing and Tinnitus Center can provide clear answers and help determine whether hearing aids or other solutions are appropriate for you.

Are hearing aids covered by insurance?

Insurance coverage for hearing aids varies depending on your individual plan. Some private insurance policies offer hearing aid benefits, while traditional Medicare typically does not.

How often should I get a hearing test?

For most adults, hearing should be evaluated every one to two years, depending on age, medical history, and any changes you notice. If you are over 50, have a history of noise exposure, experience tinnitus, or already wear hearing aids, annual testing is often recommended. Regular hearing evaluations allow us to monitor changes early and help you maintain your best possible hearing health.

How can hearing aids help with tinnitus?

Many patients experience tinnitus relief with hearing aids. By restoring access to everyday sounds, hearing aids can reduce the contrast between tinnitus and silence, making the ringing or buzzing less noticeable. Some devices also include built-in tinnitus therapy features for added support. Our audiologist will work closely with you to recommend solutions that address both your hearing needs and the impact tinnitus has on your daily life.
