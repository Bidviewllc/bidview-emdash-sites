Hearing aids are designed to do more than just make sounds louder. They’re meant to help you hear naturally and comfortably. To work their best, they need to be carefully adjusted by an audiologist to match your unique hearing needs. This is where a professional hearing aid fitting comes to mind.

A professional fitting ensures your hearing aids are both comfortable and effective.

At WNC Audiology, our experienced audiologists use advanced tools and techniques to fine-tune your devices so they feel right and sound clear in your everyday life.

Why a Professional Hearing Aid Fitting Is Important

A proper fitting makes all the difference. When hearing aids are adjusted correctly, speech is easier to follow, background noise is less distracting, and sounds feel more natural.

At WNC Audiology, we make sure your devices are set up to give you the clearest hearing possible in the places that matter most to you.

Why Should an Audiologist Fit Your Hearing Aids?

Audiologists are specialists in hearing health and know how to program your hearing aids to match your exact hearing profile. Without the right fitting, hearing aids can sound unnatural or feel uncomfortable.

Our audiologists in Asheville, NC ensure your devices fit securely and comfortably, so you can wear them all day with confidence.

What To Expect During a Hearing Aid Fitting at WNC Audiology

During your fitting, an audiologist will review your hearing test results and explain how hearing aids can support your needs.

Your hearing aids will then be programmed with specialized software. We also use Real Ear Measurement (REM), where a small microphone is placed in your ear to make sure your devices are amplifying sounds correctly.

Before you leave, we’ll teach you how to insert, remove, clean, and care for your devices. Follow-up appointments will also be scheduled so we can make adjustments once you start using them in real-world situations.

What to Expect After Your First Fitting

Your first fitting is just the beginning. As you adjust, you may notice everyday sounds (like footsteps, clocks, or your own voice) seem louder or different. This is normal, and your brain will adapt with time.

We’ll be here to support you through the process and make any changes needed so you feel comfortable and confident.

What Is Real Ear Measurement (REM)?

REM is a test that measures how your hearing aids perform inside your ear canal. A tiny microphone captures sound levels so your audiologist can adjust your devices to match your unique ear shape and needs.

Why Real Ear Measurement Matters

Everyone’s ears are different. REM ensures your hearing aids are tailored specifically for you, giving you the clearest speech, most natural sound, and best comfort possible.

At WNC Audiology, REM is a standard part of every fitting.

Adjusting to Hearing Aids

It takes time to get used to hearing aids, and that’s okay. Sounds may seem unusual at first, but with patience and practice, they will begin to feel natural again.

Tips to Help You Adjust:

  • Wear your hearing aids consistently. The more you wear them, the easier it will be for you to get used to them.
  • Start in quiet environments before moving into louder settings.
  • Be patient. Adjustment usually takes a few weeks.

Why Follow-Up Visits Are Important

Follow-up visits allow us to fine-tune your devices based on your daily experiences. These adjustments make sure your hearing aids continue to work well and feel comfortable.

Hearing Aid Fitting FAQs

Are hearing aid fittings covered by insurance?

Coverage depends on your plan. Medicare usually does not cover hearing aids, but some private insurance and veterans’ benefits may help. We recommend checking with your provider for details.

How can I make my hearing aids more comfortable?

If your devices don’t feel right, let us know. We can adjust the fit, change ear tips, or create custom molds to improve comfort.

How long does a hearing aid fitting take?

The first fitting usually takes about an hour. A few follow-up visits are typically needed to fine-tune the fit and settings.
