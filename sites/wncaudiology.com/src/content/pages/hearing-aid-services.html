Hearing Aid Selection

Finding the right hearing aid isn’t like picking an item off a shelf. It requires expert guidance. Our audiologists will recommend devices that fit your degree of hearing loss, daily activities, and budget. It’s never one-size-fits-all, it’s about what works best for you.

Hearing Test: Where It All Begins

Better hearing starts with a comprehensive evaluation. At WNC Audiology, our hearing tests may include:

  • Pure-Tone Audiometry – measures the softest sounds you can hear at different pitches.
  • Speech Audiometry – checks how well you understand spoken words.
  • Tympanometry – evaluates how well your middle ear is working.

This information helps us create a complete picture of your hearing health.

Hearing Aid Styles We Offer

We provide a wide range of modern, discreet, and powerful hearing aid styles, including:

  • Completely-in-the-Canal (CIC): Nearly invisible, for mild to moderate hearing loss.
  • Receiver-in-the-Canal (RIC): Discreet, versatile, and effective for mild to severe hearing loss.
  • In-the-Canal (ITC): Slightly larger than CIC, with more features while still discreet.
  • In-the-Ear (ITE): Easy to handle, fits fully inside the outer ear.
  • Behind-the-Ear (BTE): Reliable and powerful, suitable for most types of hearing loss.

Hearing Aid Fitting and Adjustments

Once you’ve selected your devices, we’ll make sure they fit securely and comfortably. During your fitting, we fine-tune programming so you avoid problems like feedback, whistling, or discomfort. Our goal is for you to experience natural, clear sound in every environment.

Our expert audiology care doesn’t stop at the fitting. As your hearing needs or lifestyle change, we’ll adjust and fine-tune your devices to keep them performing their best.

Education & Support

We don’t just send you home with hearing aids, we teach you how to succeed with them.

Our team will show you how to insert and remove your devices, change or charge batteries, clean them properly, and use any related apps or accessories.

We’ll also share communication tips so you feel confident in any situation.

Real-Ear Measurements (REM)

No two ears are exactly the same. That’s why we perform real-ear measurements to make sure your devices deliver the right amplification for your ear canal’s unique acoustics. This ensures you’re getting the most accurate, personalized sound possible.
