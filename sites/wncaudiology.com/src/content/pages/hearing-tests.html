When Should You Get Your Hearing Tested?

If you’ve noticed recent changes in your hearing, it’s time to schedule a test.

Hearing loss often happens slowly, which means early signs can be easy to overlook. You might struggle to follow conversations in noisy restaurants, ask people to repeat themselves more often, or feel like others are mumbling.

A professional hearing test with an audiologist provides the most accurate diagnosis and gives you peace of mind about your hearing health.

Hearing Tests We Offer at WNC Audiology

We offer a variety of tests to create a complete picture of your hearing ability. Some of the most common include:

  • Pure-Tone Audiometry – checks the faintest tones you can hear at different pitches.
  • Speech Audiometry – measures how clearly you can understand words.
  • Tympanometry – evaluates how well your middle ear is functioning.

What to Expect During Your Hearing Test

Hearing tests are simple, safe, and completely painless. Most appointments take about 30 to 45 minutes.
When you visit WNC Audiology for a hearing test, we’ll begin by reviewing your medical history and any hearing concerns you may have. We’ll also examine your ears to check for wax buildup, blockages, or signs of infection.

During the test, you’ll wear headphones and listen to a series of tones and spoken words at different volumes and frequencies. This helps us measure how well you detect and process sound.

Your Hearing Test Results

Once the test is complete, we’ll review your results with you using an audiogram, a visual chart that shows how well you hear different sounds.

If hearing loss is detected, we’ll discuss next steps. Depending on your needs, treatment options may include hearing aids, sound therapy, assistive listening devices, cochlear implants, or a medical referral.

At WNC Audiology, our goal is to help you hear more clearly so you can enjoy everyday conversations and stay connected with the people who matter most.

Frequently Asked Questions About Hearing Tests

Can I test my own hearing?

You can try online screenings, but they can’t replace the accuracy of a professional hearing test with an audiologist.

Are hearing tests safe?

Yes! Hearing tests are completely safe, non-invasive, and painless.

Are online hearing tests reliable?

Not 100%. Online hearing tests lack the detailed accuracy of a full hearing evaluation. Only a professional test performed by an audiologist can identify the exact type and degree of hearing loss.

Can I take a hearing test on my phone?

Yes, there are apps that provide simple hearing screenings, but they can’t diagnose hearing loss or identify the underlying cause. For accurate results and a complete picture of your hearing health, a professional test with an audiologist is best.

How often should I get a hearing test?

If your hearing is normal, testing every 3 to 5 years is usually enough. If you already have hearing loss, wear hearing aids, or are regularly exposed to loud environments, we recommend scheduling a hearing test once a year.
