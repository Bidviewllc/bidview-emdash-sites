Over the Counter Hearing Aids: What You Need to Know

  • October 3, 2025
  • Lisa Angelina, Au.D.
Table of Contents

Hearing loss can make everyday life harder. You may notice yourself turning up the TV, asking people to repeat themselves, or avoiding noisy places. For many people, hearing aids make a big difference. In recent years, over the counter (OTC) hearing aids have become available. You can now buy them in stores or online without seeing a hearing specialist first.

While these devices may seem like an easy solution, they are not right for everyone. At WNC Audiology, we want to help you understand what OTC hearing aids can and cannot do, so you can make the best choice for your hearing health.

What Are Over the Counter Hearing Aids?

OTC hearing aids are FDA-approved devices designed for adults with mild to moderate hearing loss. You can buy them without a prescription or hearing test. They usually come with a few settings you can adjust on your own, either through buttons or an app.

The idea is to make hearing support more affordable and accessible. But it is important to remember that these devices are limited compared to hearing aids fitted and programmed by an audiologist.

Who Might Benefit From OTC Hearing Aids?

If you have mild hearing loss and only struggle in certain situations, OTC hearing aids may give you some help. For example:

  • You have trouble hearing in small groups or restaurants.
  • You sometimes miss softer voices.
  • You need to turn the TV volume a little higher than others.

If your hearing needs are simple, these devices may improve daily conversations. But if your hearing loss is more than mild, or if you also have ringing in your ears (tinnitus), balance problems, or ear pain, OTC hearing aids may not be enough.

The Limits of Over the Counter Hearing Aids

OTC hearing aids can make sounds louder, but they cannot be adjusted for your exact hearing needs. This is where many people run into problems. Some of the limits include:

  • No professional fitting. They are not programmed to your hearing test results.
  • One-size-fits-all. Ear shapes and hearing needs are different for everyone, but OTC aids are designed for general use.
  • No medical check. An audiologist can rule out earwax buildup, infection, or other medical issues that may cause hearing problems. WNC Audiology connects you to expert audiologists in Asheville, NC.
  • May not address background noise. Many OTC devices struggle in noisy places.
  • Limited support. If you have trouble, there may not be a professional available to help you adjust them.

Because of these limits, many people who try OTC hearing aids end up frustrated and stop using them.

Why Professional Hearing Care Still Matters

At WNC Audiology, we believe hearing care is personal. A professional hearing test does more than measure what you can and cannot hear. It also helps find the cause of your hearing trouble. Sometimes the solution is as simple as removing earwax. Other times, you may need hearing aids that are carefully programmed for your ears.

When you work with an audiologist, you get:

  • A full hearing exam to understand your exact hearing needs.
  • Personalized fitting and adjustments for clear, natural sound.
  • Real Ear Measurement to make sure your hearing aids match your hearing loss.
  • Support and follow-up care, so your hearing aids keep working well over time.

Hearing aids are not just about making sounds louder. They are about helping you stay connected to family, friends, and the activities you enjoy.

Questions to Ask Before Buying OTC Hearing Aids

If you are thinking about trying OTC hearing aids, ask yourself:

  • Am I sure my hearing loss is only mild?
  • Have I had my hearing tested recently?
  • Do I want help if the devices do not work well?
  • Am I willing to adjust the devices on my own?

If you are unsure about any of these questions, it may be best to start with a professional hearing test first.
