Hearing Aid Brands We Offer

Oticon Hearing Aids

With more than a century of innovation, Oticon creates hearing aids that support how the brain naturally processes sound. Their BrainHearing™ technology reduces listening effort and improves speech understanding, even in noisy settings. Models like Oticon Intent and Oticon Real offer Bluetooth connectivity, tinnitus relief, and personalized listening. The latest Oticon Intent 2 provides even greater clarity with advanced sound personalization.

Widex Hearing Aids

Widex is known for its lifelike sound quality. Devices like the Widex Moment and Widex SmartRIC deliver fast sound processing with virtually no delay, along with tinnitus relief through Zen Therapy. Phonak Hearing Aids

Phonak leads the way with technology that makes speech clearer in any environment. Phonak Lumity enhances speech understanding, while Phonak Naída provides power for severe hearing loss. The new Audéo Sphere Infinio platform uses artificial intelligence to adapt in real time. Custom models like the Virto Titanium Infinio bring discreet, powerful options for everyday use.

Starkey Hearing Aids

Starkey combines great hearing performance with wellness features. Starkey Genesis AI and Evolv AI not only deliver clear sound but also offer fall alerts, activity tracking, and hands-free calling. Their AI-powered processors make conversations more natural and effortless.

Unitron hearing aids are known for comfort and adaptability. Their Blu platform offers Bluetooth streaming, app-based adjustments, and rechargeable options. The new Smile platform adds AI-powered sound tuning for smoother transitions in different listening environments.

Custom Ear Molds

Custom ear molds are shaped specifically for your ears, ensuring a secure fit and clear sound. They can be used for hearing aids, in-ear monitors (IEMs) for musicians, headsets, or swim plugs. Benefits include:

  • Better comfort for all-day wear
  • Reduced risk of whistling (feedback)
  • Peace of mind knowing your devices stay in place

Assistive Listening Devices (ALDs)

Sometimes, hearing aids need extra support in challenging environments. That’s where ALDs come in. These tools make listening easier in classrooms, meetings, theaters, or at home.

Examples include:

  • TV Streamers: Send sound directly to your hearing aids.
  • FM Systems: Amplify a teacher’s or speaker’s voice in group settings.
  • Personal Amplifiers: Boost sound for one-on-one conversations.
  • Hearing Loops: Work with telecoil-enabled hearing aids in public spaces.
  • Bluetooth Microphones: Stream a partner’s or colleague’s voice directly into your devices.
  • CaptionCall: A helpful phone service for people with hearing loss. It displays real-time captions of what the caller is saying on a large screen, while still letting you hear their voice.

Frequently Asked Questions

Which hearing aids are the most advanced?

That depends on your needs. Many of today’s top models include rechargeable batteries, Bluetooth streaming, AI-powered sound processing, and features that reduce background noise.

Which brands help with tinnitus?

Oticon, Widex, and Starkey all offer special tinnitus management programs built into their devices.

What hearing aids work best for severe hearing loss?

High-powered devices from Phonak, Oticon, and Starkey provide excellent options for people with severe to profound hearing loss.

Is it safe to sleep with custom earplugs?

Yes, custom earplugs designed for sleep are safe as long as they’re kept clean and used correctly.

How long do custom ear molds last?

With good care, they usually last between 2–5 years.
