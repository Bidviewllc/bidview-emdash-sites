Why Seeing a Doctor of Audiology is Important

A Doctor of Audiology is a healthcare professional trained in diagnosing and treating hearing and balance disorders. They can evaluate your hearing, explain your test results, and recommend personalized treatment options that best fit your needs.

Why Would I Need a Hearing Test?

Hearing loss often develops slowly, making it hard to notice at first. Some common signs include:

  • Difficulty hearing in noisy environments
  • Frequently asking others to repeat themselves
  • Turning up the volume on the TV or phone
  • Ringing or buzzing sounds in the ears (tinnitus)

 

At Campbell Hearing Solutions, we offer a full range of hearing tests, including:

  • Pure-tone audiometry – Measures your ability to hear different tones and pitches.
  • Speech testing – Assesses how clearly you can understand spoken words.
  • Tympanometry – Evaluates how your eardrum responds to sound and pressure.

What Are the Types of Hearing Tests?

  • Pure-Tone Audiometry – Identifies the softest sounds you can hear at different frequencies.
  • Speech Audiometry – Tests how well you recognize and understand speech at different volume levels.
  • Tympanometry – Checks for issues in the middle ear, such as fluid buildup or eardrum movement problems.

Three Main Types of Hearing Loss

  • Sensorineural Hearing Loss – Caused by damage to the inner ear or auditory nerve. This type of hearing loss is usually permanent and is often treated with hearing aids or cochlear implants.
  • Conductive Hearing Loss – Occurs when sound cannot travel through the outer or middle ear. It may be caused by ear infections, wax buildup, or other blockages and can often be treated medically or surgically.
  • Mixed Hearing Loss – A combination of sensorineural and conductive hearing loss, which may require both medical treatment and hearing technology.

Hearing Test: What to Expect

During your hearing test, you’ll wear headphones and listen to a series of tones at different volumes and pitches. The audiologist will also examine your ears and ask about your hearing history. The test is painless and typically takes 30 to 45 minutes.

Hearing Test Results

After the evaluation, the audiologist will go over your results using an audiogram, a chart that shows how well you hear across different frequencies. If hearing loss is found, they’ll discuss your treatment options, which may include hearing aids, medical follow-up, or other hearing care solutions.

Hearing Test FAQs

Can I test my own hearing?

You can try an online hearing screening, but it won’t be as detailed or accurate as a professional hearing test. Our clinic offers complete hearing evaluations for more precise results.

Will I need to do anything to prepare for a hearing test?

No special preparation is needed, but try to avoid loud noise for several hours before your appointment.

Are there any risks to hearing tests?

No—hearing tests are completely safe and painless.

How reliable are online hearing tests?

Online tests may provide a general idea of your hearing, but they cannot detect all types of hearing loss or measure hearing accurately. For the most reliable results, schedule a professional evaluation.

Can you do a hearing test on your phone?

Some phone apps offer basic hearing checks, but they don’t replace a full exam by an audiologist.

How often should I take a hearing test?

If your hearing is normal, testing every 3 to 5 years is recommended. If you have hearing loss, use hearing aids, or work in loud environments, it’s best to have your hearing checked once a year.
