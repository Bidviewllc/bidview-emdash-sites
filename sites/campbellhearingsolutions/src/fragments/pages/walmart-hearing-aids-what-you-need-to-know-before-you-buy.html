Are Walmart Hearing Aids Legitimate?

Many people don’t realize that not all devices sold as “hearing aids” are actually medical-grade. Walmart sells both FDA-registered hearing aids and personal sound amplifiers (PSAPs)—but they’re not the same thing.

Hearing Aids vs. Sound Amplifiers

  • FDA-Registered Hearing Aids are true medical devices meant to treat mild to moderate hearing loss. Some OTC options fall under this category and can be sold without a prescription.
  • PSAPs simply make all sounds louder. They do not adjust to your hearing profile, are not intended to treat hearing loss, and are not regulated as medical devices.

 

How to Tell the Difference:

  • Look for FDA registration on the label
  • Check if the packaging says “hearing aid” or “amplifier”
  • Devices under $200 are often amplifiers
  • See if the device offers programming or fitting options

Do Cheap Hearing Aids Work?

Sometimes—but only for very mild hearing loss. From an audiologist’s perspective, low-cost devices often don’t provide the quality or reliability people expect.

Common issues with cheap hearing aids:

  • Sounds too loud in quiet settings
  • Not enough clarity in noisy places
  • Whistling or feedback
  • Poor fit and comfort
  • No support if something goes wrong
