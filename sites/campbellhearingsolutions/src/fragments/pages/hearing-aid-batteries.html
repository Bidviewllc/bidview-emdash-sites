Hearing Aid Batteries

Campbell Hearing Solutions provides hearing aid batteries in Granger, IN to ensure that your hearing devices are always functioning at their best.

Hearing aid batteries are what keep your hearing aids working. Without the right battery, your hearing aids won’t perform well or last through the day. The type of battery you use can affect both how well your hearing aids work and how long they stay powered. Choosing the right battery helps with convenience, performance, and cost.

Two Types of Hearing Aid Batteries

There are two main types of hearing aid batteries: rechargeable batteries and standard disposable batteries. Each has its own advantages.

Rechargeable Batteries

Rechargeable batteries are built into your hearing aids and don’t need to be replaced often. You simply place your hearing aids in a charging dock or case at night, and they’re fully powered by morning.

Benefits of rechargeable batteries:

  • No need to regularly buy and replace batteries
  • Easy to recharge overnight
  • Environmentally friendly-less waste
  • Great for those who have trouble handling small items

 

Keep in mind: rechargeable hearing aids need to be charged daily. If you forget to charge them, they won’t work until you recharge.

Standard Disposable Batteries

Disposable hearing aid batteries, also called zinc-air batteries, are small and round. They start working when you remove a sticker that lets air in.

Benefits of disposable batteries:

  • Easy to replace when they run out
  • Useful when a power source isn’t available
  • Battery life varies based on size and hearing aid type

 

Disposable batteries are a good choice for people who travel often or don’t always have access to a charger. However, they do need to be replaced regularly, which adds to long-term cost.

Sizes of Disposable Hearing Aid Batteries

Disposable batteries come in different sizes to fit various types of hearing aids:

  • Size 10 – Smallest battery, used in tiny hearing aids; lasts about 3-5 days
  • Size 312 – Common in behind-the-ear (BTE) and in-the-ear (ITE) styles; lasts about 5-7 days
  • Size 13 – Slightly larger, used in stronger hearing aids; lasts about 6-14 days
  • Size 675 – Largest battery, used in high-powered devices; lasts about 9-20 days

Color Codes for Battery Sizes

To help you choose the right battery size, each type is color-coded on the packaging:

  • Size 10 – Yellow
  • Size 312 – Brown
  • Size 13 – Orange
  • Size 675 – Blue

How Long Do Hearing Aid Batteries Last?

Battery life depends on the size of the battery, the type of hearing aid, and how often you use it.

  • Small batteries: 3-7 days
  • Larger batteries: 6-20 days
  • Rechargeable batteries: usually last one full day, then need to be recharged nightly

 

Using features like Bluetooth or streaming can drain batteries faster.

Tips to Make Hearing Aid Batteries Last Longer

Proper Storage and Handling:

  • Keep batteries in a cool, dry place
  • Don’t store them in the refrigerator
  • Keep them away from metal objects (keys, coins) to avoid short-circuiting

 

Activating Zinc-Air Batteries:

  • Remove the sticker only when you’re ready to use the battery
  • Let it sit for 1–2 minutes before inserting it into the hearing aid—this helps activate it fully

 

Caring for Rechargeable Batteries:

  • Charge your hearing aids every night
  • Avoid overcharging—always use the charger made for your hearing aids
  • If storing hearing aids for a long time, turn them off to save battery life

When to Replace Your Batteries

You may need new batteries if:

  • Sounds become faint or unclear
  • Your hearing aid stops working
  • You hear beeping sounds (a low battery warning)
