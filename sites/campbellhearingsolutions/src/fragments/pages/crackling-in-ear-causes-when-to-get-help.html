Crackling in Ear: What It Means, Common Causes, and When to Get Help

  • David A. Campbell, M.D
  • January 23, 2026
  • Crackling in the ear is often caused by pressure problems, earwax blockage, or fluid in the middle ear. Many cases are common and treatable.
  • Simple home remedies may help at first, but ongoing crackling, discomfort, or earache should be checked by a professional.
  • An audiologist can find the cause of crackling in the ear and help prevent hearing loss, tinnitus, or infection.

Hearing a crackling in your ear can feel strange and worrying. Some people notice it when they swallow, yawn, or move their jaw. Others hear crackling sounds throughout the day, even when sitting still. While it can be harmless at times, crackling in the ear can also be a sign that something inside the ear is not working as it should.

At Campbell Hearing Solutions, we often encounter patients who are unsure if crackling in the ear is serious or just a passing issue. This guide explains the most common causes, simple ways to find relief, and when it’s time to see an audiologist or get a hearing test for answers.

What Does Crackling in the Ear Feel Like?

Crackling in the ear does not feel the same for everyone. You might notice:

  • Crackling or popping sounds
  • A feeling of fullness or blockage
  • Muffled hearing
  • Pressure that comes and goes
  • Mild earache or discomfort
  • Crackling when chewing or moving your jaw

Some people also notice ringing sounds, known as tinnitus, along with crackling. These symptoms may appear together or on their own.

Why Crackling in the Ear Happens

Your ears are made up of small, delicate parts that must work together. When one part is blocked, swollen, or irritated, it can cause crackling sounds.

Eustachian Tube Dysfunction

One of the most common causes of crackling in the ear is Eustachian tube dysfunction. The Eustachian tubes are small passages that connect the middle ear to the back of the nose and throat. They help:

  • Balance air pressure
  • Drain fluid
  • Keep the ear comfortable

When these tubes cannot open and close properly, air becomes trapped. This trapped air causes pressure, popping, and crackling in the ear.

Common triggers include:

  • Colds and sinus infections
  • Allergies
  • Changes in weather or humidity
  • Flying or altitude changes

Earwax Blockage

Earwax protects your ears, but too much earwax can cause a blockage. When wax builds up, it can block sound and movement inside the ear canal.

Signs earwax may be the cause include:

  • Crackling sounds
  • Muffled hearing
  • A plugged or full feeling
  • Ringing or tinnitus

Trying to remove earwax at home with cotton swabs can make the blockage worse. A medical evaluation is the safest next step.

Fluid in the Middle Ear

Fluid behind the eardrum often develops after a cold, allergy flare-up, or sinus infection. Even without pain, fluid can cause:

  • Crackling or bubbling sounds
  • Pressure or fullness
  • Temporary hearing changes

If fluid stays too long, it may lead to discomfort, earache, or infection.

Ear Infections and Crackling

An ear infection can also cause crackling in the ear. Infections often come with:

  • Earache or pain
  • Warmth or pressure in the ear
  • Drainage
  • Hearing changes

If crackling is paired with pain or fever, it is important to see a doctor or audiologist right away.

Jaw Problems (TMJ)

The jaw joint sits close to the ear. Problems with this joint, called TMJ disorders, can cause sounds that feel like they are coming from the ear.

Crackling linked to jaw issues may happen:

  • When chewing
  • When opening the mouth wide
  • With jaw pain or stiffness

An audiologist can help decide if the ear or jaw is the main cause.

Pressure Changes From Travel

Crackling in the ear is common during:

  • Airplane takeoff and landing
  • Driving in hilly or mountain areas
  • Elevator rides in tall buildings

These pressure changes make the ears work harder to equalize. Crackling usually goes away once pressure settles, but repeated problems may signal tube issues.

Is Crackling in the Ear Dangerous?

Most of the time, crackling in the ear is not dangerous, but it should still be watched. You should schedule a hearing check if crackling:

  • Lasts more than a week
  • Happens often
  • Comes with hearing loss
  • Causes pain, dizziness, or balance issues
  • Occurs in only one ear

Early care can prevent bigger problems later.

Simple At-Home Steps That May Help

For mild crackling in the ear, these gentle steps may help:

  • Swallow, yawn, or chew sugar-free gum
  • Drink water to stay hydrated
  • Use a warm compress near the ear
  • Take steamy showers to loosen congestion
  • Rest if you are sick

Avoid forcing your ears to “pop,” as this can irritate the eardrum.

When to Avoid At-Home Treatment

Do not try home remedies if you have:

  • Ear pain
  • Drainage or fluid from the ear
  • Sudden hearing loss
  • Dizziness or spinning

These signs need professional care.

Treatment Options for Crackling in the Ear

Treatment depends on the cause. Options may include:

Professional Ear Evaluation

A qualified medical provider can determine whether wax buildup is part of the problem and recommend the right treatment.

Middle-Ear Testing

This checks how well the eardrum and middle ear are moving.

Allergy or Sinus Care Guidance

Reducing swelling can help the ears open naturally.

Hearing Monitoring

If hearing changes are found, we track them over time.

Referrals When Needed

If a medical issue is suspected, we guide you to the right provider.

Crackling in the Ear and Hearing Loss

Sometimes crackling in the ear is an early sign of hearing changes. Even mild hearing loss can affect how sound travels through the ear.

A hearing test can:

  • Measure hearing levels
  • Check how well you hear speech
  • Spot early changes before they worsen

Early detection leads to better outcomes.

Children and Crackling in the Ear

Kids can also experience crackling in the ear, often from:

  • Ear infections
  • Fluid buildup
  • Frequent colds

If a child complains of ear sounds, trouble hearing, or ear fullness, a hearing check is important.

Preventing Crackling in the Ear

While not all causes can be prevented, these habits help protect ear health:

  • Avoid cotton swabs in the ear canal
  • Treat colds and allergies early
  • Wear hearing protection in loud places
  • Dry ears gently after swimming or bathing
  • Get routine hearing checks

When to Schedule a Hearing Evaluation

Make an appointment if you notice:

  • Ongoing crackling in the ear
  • Hearing that feels muffled
  • Trouble understanding speech
  • Ringing or buzzing sounds
  • Ear pressure that will not go away

These signs are your body’s way of asking for help.
