Types of Hearing Protection

Ready-Made Hearing Protection

These pre-molded options are convenient and budget-friendly, though they may not offer the best comfort or fit for long-term use.

  • Earplugs – Inserted into the ear canal.
    • Pros: Affordable and portable.
    • Cons: May provide less comfort and a less secure fit.
  • Semi-Insert Earplugs – Connected by a band and rest at the entrance of the ear canal.
    • Pros: Reusable and easy to wear.
    • Cons: Lower noise reduction.
  • Earmuffs – Cover the entire ear to block outside noise.
    • Pros: Strong overall protection.
    • Cons: Can feel bulky or uncomfortable with extended use.

Custom Hearing Protection

Made using impressions of your ears, custom protection offers a superior fit, better noise reduction, and all-day comfort.

  • Custom Earplugs – Great for musicians, workers, and everyday protection.
    • Pros: Comfortable, durable, and secure.
    • Cons: Requires a custom fitting.
  • Impulse Filter Earplugs – Designed for hunters to block sudden loud sounds like gunfire while allowing softer ambient sounds.
    • Pros: Smart protection.
    • Cons: Not intended for continuous loud noise.
  • Sleep Plugs – Soft, flexible earplugs made for nighttime use.
    • Pros: Ideal for side sleepers and blocking noise.
    • Cons: May reduce awareness of alarms or essential sounds.
  • In-Ear Monitors – High-fidelity devices used by musicians and performers.
    • Pros: Outstanding sound clarity.
    • Cons: Higher cost compared to standard plugs.
  • SoundGear Digital Protection – Digital hearing protection that reduces loud noises while preserving clarity.
    • Pros: Ideal for shooting and noisy environments.
    • Cons: Premium cost.

Types of Custom Ear Molds

We offer several material options for your custom ear molds:

  • Vinyl – Soft and flexible, perfect for children or sensitive ears.
    • Pros: Comfortable and hypoallergenic.
    • Cons: Less durable over time.
  • Silicone – Water-resistant, flexible, and ideal for active users.
    • Pros: Long-lasting and durable.
    • Cons: Harder to adjust once molded.
  • Acrylic – Hard, lightweight, and easy to clean.
    • Pros: Great clarity and ease of use.
    • Cons: May not be best for those with sensitive ears.

 

Custom molds can be used for hearing aids, swimming, sleeping, or sound protection.

How Are Custom Ear Molds Made?

Our Granger, IN audiologists begin with a quick and painless ear impression. That impression is then sent to a lab where your chosen material is used to craft a mold that fits your ear perfectly. Custom molds provide a better seal, increased comfort, and greater protection compared to generic options.

How Long Do Custom Earplugs Last?

With proper care, custom earplugs can last 3 to 5 years. Their lifespan depends on how often they’re used and the material. Regular cleaning and periodic checkups help ensure a good fit-especially for growing children or adults with ear shape changes.
