Hearing Assessments

The first step to better hearing is a thorough hearing evaluation. This helps identify the type and degree of hearing loss. Common tests include:

  • Pure-Tone Audiometry – Measures how well you hear different tones and pitches.
  • Speech Audiometry – Evaluates how clearly you understand speech in quiet and noisy settings.
  • Tympanometry – Assesses the health of your eardrum and middle ear function.

Hearing Aid Styles

Hearing aids come in different styles to suit various hearing needs and preferences:

  • Completely-in-the-Canal (CIC): Very small and discreet, best for mild to moderate hearing loss.
  • In-the-Canal (ITC): Sits partially in the ear canal.
  • In-the-Ear (ITE): Fits into the outer ear and is easier to handle.
  • Behind-the-Ear (BTE): Worn behind the ear with a small tube to the ear canal.

Real Ear Measurements

Real ear measurement testing ensures your hearing aids are delivering the correct amplification levels for your ears. A small microphone is placed in the ear canal to verify the device’s performance in real-time.

Why Real Ear Measurement Matters:

This precise testing helps ensure your hearing aids are accurately programmed to provide the best speech clarity and sound quality possible.
