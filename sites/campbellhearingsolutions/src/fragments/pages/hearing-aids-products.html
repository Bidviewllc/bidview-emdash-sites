Phonak Hearing Aids

Phonak is one of the most trusted hearing aid brands in the world. The company has been helping people hear better for over 75 years. Phonak is known for making smart, easy-to-use hearing aids with helpful features to make hearing easier for people of all ages.

Oticon Hearing Aids

Oticon has been helping people hear better since 1904. Their hearing aids use BrainHearing™ technology to support the way your brain naturally understands sound. Popular models include Oticon Real and Oticon More, which offer excellent sound clarity and noise reduction.

Widex Hearing Aids

Widex has been making hearing aids since 1956. Their Widex Moment and Widex SmartRIC hearing aids reduce sound delay, so voices and noises sound more real and clear. Widex also offers options for tinnitus relief.

Starkey Hearing Aids

Starkey hearing aids offer features like fall detection, health tracking, and tinnitus relief. Popular models include Starkey Genesis AI and Evolv AI, which are smart and easy to use with your smartphone.

Signia Hearing Aids

Signia hearing aids are known for advanced sound features and stylish designs. They offer rechargeable batteries, Bluetooth, and clear speech in noisy areas. Signia also has discreet options that are almost invisible in the ear.

FAQs:

What is the most advanced hearing aid?

Many hearing aids today come with advanced features like clear speech enhancement, Bluetooth connection, and rechargeable batteries. Different brands offer different benefits. The best hearing aid for you depends on your hearing needs and lifestyle. An audiologist can help guide you toward the most advanced option that’s right for you.

Which brand of hearing aid is best for tinnitus?

Several hearing aid brands include features to help with tinnitus, such as sound therapy or masking sounds. But what works well for one person might not be the best fit for another. The best way to find relief is to speak with an audiologist. They can recommend hearing aids and treatment options based on your symptoms.

Which hearing aid is best for severe loss?

There are many powerful hearing aids designed for people with severe hearing loss. These models offer strong amplification and clear sound. But every person’s hearing is different. The best hearing aid for you depends on your specific hearing test results. Your audiologist will help you find the right solution that meets your needs.

Custom Hearing Protection

Custom earplugs are made to fit your ears exactly. They offer better comfort and sound protection than regular earplugs. These can be used for loud work areas, concerts, shooting sports, or swimming. Custom ear protection helps protect your hearing without blocking out all sound. Talk to our audiologists about what type of ear protection is best for your needs.

Custom Ear Molds

Custom ear molds are soft molds made to fit the shape of your ear. They are most often used for custom hearing aids. These molds give a better fit and help keep sound from leaking out. Ear molds can also be used for earphones, hearing protection, or swim plugs. An audiologist takes a quick ear impression, then sends it to a lab to create a mold that fits you perfectly. This helps make sure your hearing device is both comfortable and effective.

Custom Ear Molds for Musicians

Musicians need to protect their hearing while still hearing music clearly. Custom ear molds can be used to create musician earplugs or in-ear monitors. These help block out harmful noise while keeping sound quality clear. Custom molds also give a comfortable, secure fit during performances or rehearsals.

FAQs:

Is it OK to sleep with ear plugs in?

Yes, you can sleep with earplugs, but make sure they fit well and keep your ears clean to avoid irritation or infection.

How long do ear molds last?

Custom ear molds usually last 2-5 years, depending on how often you use them and how well you care for them.

Why are ear molds used in hearing aid fitting?

Ear molds give a better fit and sound quality. They also help keep the hearing aid in place and reduce feedback or whistling.

Assistive Listening Devices

Assistive Listening Devices help people hear better in certain situations, like watching TV, talking on the phone, or being in a meeting or classroom. ALDs can work with or without hearing aids. Some common types include TV streamers, FM systems, personal amplifiers, and Bluetooth microphones.

Working with an audiologist can help you choose the right devices for your lifestyle. For example, if you often have trouble hearing in meetings or at church, an FM or loop system might help. If you have hearing aids, many ALDs can connect to them wirelessly for better sound.

Assistive devices can make a big difference in your daily life-helping you feel more connected and confident.

 

FAQs:

What is the most commonly used assistive listening device?

Personal amplifiers and TV streamers are very common. They help people hear better in noisy or distant situations.

Can you turn an iPhone into a listening device?

Yes, iPhones have a feature called Live Listen that can send sound from your phone to your hearing aids or headphones.

Where are assistive listening devices required?

Many public places, like theaters, churches, and government buildings, must provide assistive listening systems by law so people with hearing loss can hear clearly.
