How to Service Hearing Aids: A Simple Guide to Better Hearing and Longer Device Life

  • David A. Campbell, M.D
  • February 11, 2026
  • Regular hearing aid service keeps sound clear and helps devices last longer.
  • Most hearing aid problems are caused by wax, moisture, or poor cleaning.
  • Professional service at Campbell Hearing Solutions can prevent costly hearing aid repairs.

Hearing aids work hard every day. They sit in the ear, handle sound, and deal with moisture, earwax, and dirt. Knowing how to service hearing aids is important if you want them to work well and last as long as possible. When hearing aids are not cleaned or checked often, they can stop working, sound weak, or break down sooner than expected.

This guide explains how to service hearing aids at home, when to seek professional help, and how Campbell Hearing Solutions in Granger, Indiana can support your hearing health.

Why Hearing Aid Service Matters

Hearing aids are small devices, but they are powerful tools. They help you hear speech, stay connected to loved ones, and feel safer in daily life.

Without proper service:

  • Sound quality can drop
  • Batteries may drain faster
  • Moisture can damage parts
  • Earwax can block sound

Regular service protects your investment and your hearing.

Common Problems Caused by Poor Hearing Aid Care

Many hearing aid issues are not caused by the device itself. They are caused by buildup or damage over time.

Common problems include:

  • No sound or weak sound
  • Whistling or feedback
  • Devices cutting in and out
  • Short battery life
  • Pain or itching in the ear

Most of these problems can be avoided with basic care and routine service.

Daily Hearing Aid Care: What You Can Do at Home

Daily care is the first step in learning how to service hearing aids properly.

Wipe Hearing Aids Every Night

Use a soft, dry cloth to wipe:

  • The outside of the hearing aid
  • The ear mold or dome

This removes sweat, wax, and dirt before they build up.

Open the Battery Door or Power Off Devices

At night:

  • Turn off your hearing aids
  • Open the battery door (if applicable)

This lets moisture escape and saves battery power.

Store Hearing Aids in a Safe Place

Keep hearing aids:

  • In a dry case
  • Away from pets and children
  • Off bathroom counters

Moisture and accidents are common causes of damage.

Weekly Hearing Aid Service Tasks

Weekly care goes a step further and keeps sound clear.

Check for Earwax Buildup

Look at:

  • The speaker or receiver
  • The wax guard
  • The ear mold opening

Wax is one of the most common causes of hearing aid failure.

Clean Ear Molds or Domes

If your hearing aids have removable ear molds or domes:

  • Remove them carefully
  • Wash with warm water and mild soap
  • Let them dry fully before reattaching

Never wash the hearing aid itself.

Replace Wax Guards When Needed

Wax guards protect the speaker from earwax.

Signs you need a new wax guard:

  • Sound is weak
  • Hearing aid seems blocked
  • Cleaning does not help

Wax guards are simple to replace but easy to forget.

Monthly Hearing Aid Service Tips

Once a month, take time to fully inspect your devices.

Check for Cracks or Damage

Look for:

  • Cracks in the shell
  • Loose parts
  • Bent battery doors

If you see damage, stop using the device and seek help.

Test Sound Quality

Ask yourself:

  • Does speech sound clear?
  • Is volume steady?
  • Are both hearing aids working the same?

Changes may signal a need for professional service.

Moisture: A Major Enemy of Hearing Aids

Hearing aids and moisture do not mix.

Moisture comes from:

  • Sweat
  • Humidity
  • Rain
  • Bathrooms

Even small amounts can damage internal parts.

Use a Drying System

A hearing aid dryer or dehumidifier:

  • Pulls moisture out overnight
  • Extends device life
  • Reduces repair needs

This is especially helpful in warmer months.

How to Service Rechargeable Hearing Aids

Rechargeable hearing aids need special care.

Tips include:

  • Charge every night
  • Keep charging contacts clean
  • Avoid extreme heat
  • Do not overcharge with non-approved chargers

If charging seems slow or inconsistent, service may be needed. Campbell Hearing Solutions connects you to expert audiologists in Granger, IN.

How to Service Hearing Aids With Batteries

If your hearing aids use disposable batteries:

  • Keep batteries clean and dry
  • Remove dead batteries right away
  • Store batteries at room temperature

Battery issues often look like hearing aid problems.

When Home Care Is Not Enough

Even with good daily care, hearing aids still need professional service.

You should seek help if:

  • Sound does not improve after cleaning
  • Devices stop working
  • Hearing aids cause discomfort
  • Feedback continues

Trying to fix internal parts yourself can cause more damage.

At Campbell Hearing Solutions, professional hearing aid service includes:

  • Part replacement if needed

How Often Should Hearing Aids Be Professionally Serviced?

Most patients benefit from:

  • Professional cleaning every 6 months
  • Hearing checks once a year
  • Immediate service when problems arise

Hearing needs can change over time.

Why Regular Service Improves Hearing Results

Serviced hearing aids:

  • Sound clearer
  • Work more reliably
  • Last longer
  • Support better hearing outcomes

Clear sound helps the brain stay active and engaged.

Hearing Aid Service and Ear Health

Clean hearing aids support healthy ears.

Poorly maintained devices can cause:

  • Ear infections
  • Skin irritation
  • Itching or redness

Professional service includes checking ear health, not just devices.

Hearing Aid Service for Seniors

Older adults may need more frequent service because:

  • Skin produces more wax
  • Vision and hand strength may change
  • Devices may be worn longer each day

Local support makes care easier and safer.

Hearing Aid Service for Active Adults

If you are active or outdoors often:

  • Sweat and dirt increase buildup
  • Moisture risk is higher

Regular service helps keep devices working in all conditions.

Signs Your Hearing Aids Need Immediate Service

Call for help if:

  • Hearing aid stops working suddenly
  • There is pain or drainage from the ear
  • Sound cuts in and out
  • Battery drains too fast

Quick service prevents bigger problems.
